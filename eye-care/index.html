      What is Dry Eye?

      Our eyes need both the water and the oil in our tears to stay healthy and comfortable. When the natural tear composition of our eyes is disrupted, it causes them to feel dry and irritated. Many people suffer from dry eye, but the most common forms affect both the watery and oily layers.

      Vanguard Pharmacy has a variety of products available  to soothe its symptoms and help you feel more comfortable; but if you’re unsure which type of dry eye you have, choose a drop that treats both layers like Systane® Complete. Systane is the #1 Doctor & Pharmacist recommended brand in the Dry Eyes category in Canada. 

      Here are some of the most common dry eye symptoms: Dryness, Scratchy sensation, Stinging, Burning, Excess tearing, Blurred vision, Pain, Redness.

      Help manage dry eye symptoms with these tips:

      • Blink regularly & stay hydrated.

      • Get Omega-3 from fish or supplements.

      • 20/20/20 rule: Every 20 minutes look at an object 20 feet away for 20 seconds.

      • Maintain good lid hygiene with Systane® Lid Wipes.

      • Choose a lubricating drop that meets your needs, such as Systane® Complete.

      Advanced dry eye relief.

      Gel drops and ointments are specifically designed to provide lasting relief from more advanced symptoms. Their thicker formulations lubricate the eye’s surface, protecting it from further damage.

      While gels and ointments provide added lubrication, some are designed for nighttime use as they can cause some blur or significant visual impairment. Ensure you follow the label closely if you need to see clearly after application.

      • Redness in the white of the eyes.
      • Watery Eyes.
      • Itch or gritty feeling in the eye.
      • Thick and yellow-white discharge from the eye.

      Help treat bacterial Pink Eye with these tips

      • Get fast relief with an antibiotic eye drop without a prescription by using an effective treatment such as POLYSPORIN® Eye and Ear Drops.*

      Revive Dry Eyes from Allergies

      Allergic rhinitis, also called hay fever or pollinosis, literally means "allergic nose inflammation". Most people with allergic rhinitis know they have it, although it can sometimes be confused with the common cold.
      Read More

      • Dry eye is a condition caused by reduced production or increased evaporation of tears, which leads to eye irritation.

        • Computer use – When working at a computer or using a smartphone or other portable digital device, we tend to blink our eyes less fully and less frequently. This leads to greater tear evaporation and increased risk of dry eye symptoms.
        • Health conditions – Certain diseases such as diabetes, thyroid-associated conditions, lupus, rheumatoid arthritis and others can contribute to dry eye problems.
        • Medications – Many prescription and nonprescription medicines increase the risk of dry eye symptoms, including antihistamines, antidepressants, certain blood pressure medications such as beta-blockers or diuretics and birth control pills.
        • Allergies – Allergies can cause dry eye, taking antihistamines to relieve allergies can also cause dry eye.
        • Use of Contact lenses
        • Aging – becomes more common as you age, especially after age 50.
        • Menopause – Post-menopausal women are at greater risk of dry eye than men of the same age.
        • Indoor environment – Air conditioning, ceiling fans and forced air heating systems all can decrease indoor humidity. This can hasten tear evaporation, causing dry eye symptoms.
        • Outdoor environment – Dry climates, high altitudes and dry or windy conditions increase dry eye risks.
        • Air travel – The air in the cabins of airplanes is extremely dry and can lead to dry eye problems, especially among frequent flyers.
        • Wearing a mask – Many masks, can dry the eyes by forcing air out the top of the mask and over the surface of the eye. Wearing glasses with a mask can direct the air over the eyes even more.
